# calories

## general
1g of proteins = 4kcal
1g of carbs = 4kcal
1g of fat = 4.9kcal

## norm
1/3 of daily norm is spent on internal processes.

- talk when eating 86kcal/h
- reading 92kcal/h
- reading aloud 116kcal/h
- singing 125kcal/h
- studying (seatting) 133kcal/h
- walking 206kcal/h

## stomach
### acid
Acid is neutrolized by **pancreas** with **bicarbonate** (aka **soda**).
**Heartburn** is caused by acid from stomach that comes up to *esophagus*.

### ferments
**Ferments** are proteins inside our organism which do some work,  
e.g. break down carbs.

## intestine
Contains a lot of bacterias which:
- produce **B**, **K**, **PP** vitamins
- neutralize toxins
- neutralize methanol (with ethanol)
- produce **immunoglobulin A**
- break down **strach**

Bacterias break down everything, the results are delivered with  
blood to cells, where they are moleculary burned with oxygen.

# myths

## cholesterol
Is contained only in animal products. There is no **C** in oil.
80% of **C** in organism is produced by our own liver.  

## drinking during the food intake
Stomack's `Ph = 2`, it's almost impossible to dilute it with water  
(1-1.5l of water without brakes will decrease Ph on 1).
Water stays in stomach for 15-20 min, food for 3-4 hours.

## meal time
It is safe to eat after 18:00.

Is an aminoacid and contained in anything that is produced from  
protein.
*Parmezan* contains 18% of **MG**. All marinated products contain **MG**.

# vitamins

## definition
There are 4 criteria:
- organic
- effective even in small doses
- crucial for live
- can't be produced (or produced in amount that is not enough) by our own organism

Any substance that meets these criteria is called **vitamin**.
There are only 13 vitamins.

## role
Vitamins are required by [fements](digestation.md#ferments) to work properly  
(i.e. vitamins are like tools, catalysts).
