# antiseptic soap
Soap cleaning our skin with moleculas that are hydrophobic  
on one end (attach to all dirty particles) and  
hydrophillic on the other end (attach to water).
They are **surfacants** (surface active agents).  
Combining with **fat** they form **micells**.  
**Micalls** are not transparent, so effective soap must  
produce milk-alike substance when is mixed with **fat**.

All anticeptics additions are not effective, as pure soap  
is already effective by its nature. However, anticeptics  
(e.g. **trinicillin**) which claimed to kill 99.99% of all  
bacterias can have negative effect, because the rest of  
0.01% without competiton can reproduce themselves without  
control.
